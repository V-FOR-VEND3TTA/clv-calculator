Customer Lifetime Value FAQs

Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) is a prediction of the total net profit a business can make from a single customer throughout their entire relationship.

CLTV is calculated by multiplying the average purchase value by the purchase frequency and multiplying that by the customer lifespan. You can then multiply by your profit margin to estimate net CLTV.

CLTV helps determine the maximum amount you can spend to acquire a customer (CAC) while remaining profitable. Ideally, CLTV should be at least three times the CAC for long-term success.
